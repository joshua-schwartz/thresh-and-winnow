Competing as a Minority Owned Small Business

2.19.2016 Josh Schwartz
Minority business owners are outpacing their non-minority counterparts at more than twice the national rate.

Minority businesses are fueling small business growth in the United States. The number of minority-owned businesses is experiencing rapid growth in part because their demographic is growing. The 2010 census reported that minority business owners were outpacing their non-minority counterparts at more than twice the national rate. That same census also predicted that by the year 2017, the Hispanic population will be 59 million, the Black population will be 41 million, and the Asian population will be 16 million.

The spending power of the minority population within the U.S. is unprecedented and encourages business ownership. A report by the Selig Center for Economic Growth stated that in 2013 Hispanic consumers spent $1.3 trillion, Black consumers spent $1 trillion, Asian consumers spent $713 billion, and Native Americans spent $96 billion.

Despite those impressive numbers, minority-owned businesses still struggle to compete in the marketplace, and without fair competition, a lot of them will not survive. So if you are a small minority-owned business, how can you compete?

First, know that you can compete. There are many tactics and strategies that you can put in place to compete, thrive, and win. Here are five things you can do:

Become certified as a minority-owned business.

As discussed in a previous Kabbage blog, minority companies should take the steps to become certified. To learn more, be sure to read the blog and visit the sites for the National Supplier Diversity Council and the Minority Business Development Agency. Taking this step will ensure fair competition and allow your business to bid on various state and federal contracts. Needless to say – it’s a great way to get your foot in the door.

Develop and cultivate business relationships and strategic partnerships

Developing and cultivating business relationships is crucial to business growth, especially for small and minority businesses. This is a sound strategy because you will meet people who make the purchasing decisions within their individual companies. As you set out on this journey, you will often meet other small business owners who can introduce you to people who need your products and services. Business owners are discovering that by nurturing business relationships, they are able to develop strategic partnerships as well.

Many small businesses are implementing networking strategies into their marketing plans. Here are a few things that you can implement into your business:

1. Identify networking groups and events in your area. Contact your local Chamber of Commerce and other business associations. As you meet business people in your area, they will begin to invite you to other networking events.

2. Use LinkedIn. This is a great tool – first, to find people to network with both online and in person; second, to develop a LinkedIn strategy to use in your sales and marketing processes. Follow up with individuals you have met and connect with them.

3. Provide value first. Take the time to learn about other businesses and discuss how you can help them by referring or connecting them with others that they want to target.

Market your size.

Although you want to grow and be successful, it’s okay to be small. Market the size of your business. Not every customer wants to deal with a huge conglomerate.

For some businesses this may mean that you market a niche or offer your services as boutique or customized. Being a boutique and/or offering customized services will enable you to be closer to your customers. This allows you to receive immediate and direct feedback and implement changes as needed. Celebrating the size of your company also allows you to be responsive to your clients’ needs, which is a big advantage that you have over larger firms.

Develop a strategic online marketing campaign.

Innovation has made it possible for small and minority-owned businesses to compete on a big scale. Small businesses can implement a sound online marketing strategy through valuable, relevant, and consistent content.

First, develop an email campaign. According to 11 Email Marketing Solutions for Small Business, "studies have shown that email marketing is the most effective way to reach and retain customers." There are companies that offer small businesses valuable features for effective email marketing campaigns, such as MailChimp, AWeber, InfusionSoft, and Constant Contact. Using any of these companies is a way to reach customers in a systematic way without breaking your budget. For example, MailChimp is free for up to 2,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails a month. After you have reached that capacity plans, start at $10 per month.

The use of Social Media platforms has changed the way businesses interact with prospective and existing clients. Implementing a Social Media campaign into your marketing strategies will allow your business to compete. First, you will have to research where your target customers are on Social Media so that you can engage with them. Second, develop a Social Media editorial calendar so that you can schedule all of your content as part of your strategy. A good tool to use for this is Hootsuite.

A blog is a great way to attract new customers and increase your business visibility. By employing this tactic, you will also increase your rankings on search engines without going over your marketing budget. A blog will also put you in the position to become a thought leader and an expert in your industry. Publishing your blog content on your social media pages will also increase your fans and followers as they continually share the content.

Business Accelerator Programs.

Tapping into resources that have been made available for small businesses is a great way to learn from the best, grow, and compete with larger companies. There are great programs that offer valuable training and mentorship. One such program is the Accelerate with Google Academy. The academy is a 12 week program that helps businesses start marketing online. Participants learn how to:

1. Use professional marketing techniques to attract more pre-qualified leads.

2. Create a great website landing page to attract more customers.

3. Use online ads (Google AdWords) to bring traffic to your website.

4. Analyze your marketing.

Take a look at Accelerate with Google Academy and research other programs that can help your business compete with businesses large and small.
